---
description: Find simplest explanation that fits all facts
argument-hint: [situation or leave blank for current context]
---

<objective>
Apply Occam's Razor to $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no arguments provided).

Among competing explanations, prefer the one with fewest assumptions. Simplest doesn't mean easiest — it means fewest moving parts.
</objective>

<process>
1. List all plausible explanations or approaches
2. For each, count the assumptions required
3. Check which assumptions have evidence
4. Eliminate explanations requiring unsupported assumptions
5. Select the simplest that fits all known facts
</process>

<output_format>
**Situation:** [what we're explaining or deciding]

**Candidate Explanations:**
1. [explanation] — Assumptions: [list] — Evidence: [supported/unsupported]
2. [explanation] — Assumptions: [list] — Evidence: [supported/unsupported]
3. [explanation] — Assumptions: [list] — Evidence: [supported/unsupported]

**Simplest Valid Explanation:**
[the one with fewest unsupported assumptions that still fits all facts]

**Why It Wins:**
[what it explains without needing extra machinery]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Enumerates genuinely plausible options (not strawmen)
- Makes assumptions explicit and countable
- Distinguishes supported from unsupported assumptions
- Chosen explanation fits all known facts (not just most)
- Reduces complexity without losing explanatory power
</success_criteria>
