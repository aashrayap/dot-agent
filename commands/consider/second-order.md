---
description: Think through consequences of consequences
argument-hint: [decision or leave blank for current context]
---

<objective>
Apply second-order thinking to $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no arguments provided).

Don't stop at "what happens next." Ask "and then what?" to trace causal chains and find hidden consequences.
</objective>

<process>
1. State the decision or action under consideration
2. Map first-order consequences (immediate, obvious)
3. For each first-order effect, ask "and then what?" — map second-order consequences
4. Extend to third-order where the chain is strong
5. Identify consequences that shift the overall evaluation
</process>

<output_format>
**Decision/Action:** [what's being considered]

**First-Order (immediate):**
- [consequence 1]
- [consequence 2]

**Second-Order (triggered by first):**
- [consequence 1] → leads to [second-order effect]
- [consequence 2] → leads to [second-order effect]

**Third-Order (downstream):**
- [if the chain extends meaningfully]

**Hidden Consequences:**
[Effects that shift whether this is a good idea]

**Verdict:**
[Does this compound positively or have hidden costs?]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Goes genuinely deeper, not just restating first-order effects
- Identifies feedback loops (positive or negative)
- Surfaces unintended consequences
- Distinguishes actions that compound well from those with hidden costs
- Prevents "seemed like a good idea at the time" decisions
</success_criteria>
