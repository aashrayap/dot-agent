description: "Think backwards: what guarantees failure? Then avoid it. Usage: /consider:inversion [topic]"
---

Apply inversion thinking to: $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no topic given).

<objective>
Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "what would guarantee failure?" List all failure modes, then build a success plan by systematically avoiding each one. Surfaces risks that optimistic planning misses.
</objective>

<process>
1. State the goal clearly
2. Invert: "What would GUARANTEE failure at this?"
3. List every failure mode — be thorough and uncomfortable
4. For each failure mode, define the avoidance strategy
5. What's left after avoiding all obvious failures = your success plan
6. Identify remaining risks that inversion didn't catch
</process>

<output_format>
**Goal:** [what you're trying to achieve]

**Guaranteed Failure Modes:**
1. [way to fail] — Avoid by: [specific action]
2. ...

**Anti-Goals (never do these):**
- ...

**Success by Avoidance:**
[The plan that emerges from systematically avoiding failure]

**Remaining Risk:**
[What's left that inversion didn't surface — unknowns, external factors]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- At least 5 failure modes listed (most people stop at 2-3)
- Failure modes include uncomfortable truths, not just technical risks
- Each avoidance strategy is specific and actionable
- Remaining risk section is honest, not empty
</success_criteria>
