---
description: Prioritize by urgent vs important
argument-hint: [tasks or leave blank for current context]
---

<objective>
Apply the Eisenhower Matrix to $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no arguments provided).

Categorize by two axes: Important (contributes to long-term goals) and Urgent (demands immediate attention).
</objective>

<process>
1. List all tasks, items, or considerations in scope
2. Evaluate each on importance (long-term value) and urgency (time pressure)
3. Place in the appropriate quadrant
4. Assign specific actions per quadrant
</process>

<output_format>
|  | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|---|---|---|
| **Important** | **Q1 — Do First:** [items + deadlines] | **Q2 — Schedule:** [items + when] |
| **Not Important** | **Q3 — Delegate:** [items + to whom/minimize] | **Q4 — Eliminate:** [items to drop] |

**Immediate Focus:**
[The single most important action right now]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Correctly distinguishes urgent from important (they're different)
- Q1 has specific deadlines and actions
- Q2 items get scheduled (not just "do later")
- Q3 items have delegation targets or time-boxing
- Q4 items are explicitly dropped
- Reduces overwhelm by creating clear hierarchy
</success_criteria>
