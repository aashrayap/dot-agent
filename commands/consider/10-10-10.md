argument-hint: [decision or leave blank for current context]

<objective>
Apply the 10/10/10 rule to $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no arguments provided).

How will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes, 10 months, and 10 years? Surfaces where short-term thinking hijacks decisions.
</objective>

<process>
1. State the decision and options clearly
2. For each option, evaluate: how will I feel in 10 minutes?
3. How will I feel in 10 months?
4. How will I feel in 10 years?
5. Identify conflicts between timeframes
6. Weight recommendation toward longer horizons
</process>

<output_format>
**Decision:** [what's being decided]

**Option A: [description]**
- 10 minutes: [emotional/practical impact]
- 10 months: [medium-term consequence]
- 10 years: [long-term consequence]

**Option B: [description]**
- 10 minutes: [emotional/practical impact]
- 10 months: [medium-term consequence]
- 10 years: [long-term consequence]

**Time Conflicts:**
[Where short-term and long-term pull in different directions]

**Recommendation:**
[Weighted toward longer horizons, with reasoning]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Distinguishes temporary discomfort from lasting regret
- Makes long-term consequences tangible
- Identifies where present bias is distorting the decision
- Recommendation weighted toward 10-year perspective
- Acknowledges short-term costs honestly
</success_criteria>
