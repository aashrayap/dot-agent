description: "Find the 20% that drives 80% of results. Cut scope ruthlessly. Usage: /consider:pareto [topic]"
---

Apply Pareto analysis to: $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no topic given).

<objective>
List all factors/options/features, estimate relative impact, find the vital few that drive most of the value. Explicitly deprioritize the rest. Designed for when you're overwhelmed with options or scope.
</objective>

<process>
1. List ALL factors, options, or items under consideration
2. For each, estimate relative impact (high / medium / low) with brief reasoning
3. Rank from highest impact to lowest
4. Draw the line: which ~20% accounts for ~80% of the value?
5. Name the vital few with specific next actions
6. Name the trivial many and explicitly deprioritize them
</process>

<output_format>
**All Factors:**
| # | Factor | Impact | Reasoning |
|---|--------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | ...    | High   | ...       |
| 2 | ...    | Low    | ...       |

**Vital Few (focus here):**
1. [factor] — Action: [specific next step]
2. [factor] — Action: [specific next step]
3. [factor] — Action: [specific next step]

**Trivial Many (deprioritize):**
- [factor] — why it can wait or be dropped
- ...

**Bottom Line:** [one sentence on where to focus]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- All factors listed first, not just the obvious winners
- Impact estimates have reasoning, not just labels
- Vital few is genuinely small (3-5 items max)
- Trivial many are explicitly named — not just omitted
- Reduces decision fatigue: the reader knows exactly what to do next
</success_criteria>
