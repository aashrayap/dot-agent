<objective>
Apply SWOT analysis to $ARGUMENTS (or the current discussion if no arguments provided).

Map the full picture: internal factors you control (strengths/weaknesses) and external factors you don't (opportunities/threats).
</objective>

<process>
1. Define what's being evaluated
2. Map internal strengths (advantages you control)
3. Map internal weaknesses (disadvantages you control)
4. Identify external opportunities (favorable conditions)
5. Identify external threats (unfavorable conditions)
6. Form strategies that connect multiple quadrants
</process>

<output_format>
|  | **Helpful** | **Harmful** |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths:** [list] | **Weaknesses:** [list] |
| **External** | **Opportunities:** [list] | **Threats:** [list] |

**Strategic Moves:**
- SO (use strength to capture opportunity): [action]
- WO (address weakness to unlock opportunity): [action]
- ST (use strength to counter threat): [action]
- WT (mitigate weakness exposed by threat): [action]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Correctly distinguishes internal vs external factors
- Specific enough to be actionable (not generic platitudes)
- Strategies connect across quadrants
- Gives clear direction on where to act
- Acknowledges both potential and risk
</success_criteria>
